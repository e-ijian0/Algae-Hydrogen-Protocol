# Algae Cultivation Protocol

This document details the step-by-step process for cultivating algae optimized for hydrogen production.

## 1. Algae Strain Selection
- **Recommended strain**: *Chlamydomonas reinhardtii* (wild-type CC‑124 or CC‑503)
- Alternative strains with high hydrogen yield: *Scenedesmus obliquus*, *Chlorella vulgaris*
- Use axenic cultures to avoid contamination.

## 2. Photobioreactor Design
- **Type**: Flat-panel airlift photobioreactor (FP‑APBR)
- **Material**: UV‑stabilized polycarbonate or glass
- **Working volume**: 5 L (bench‑scale) to 50 L (pilot‑scale)
- **Light path**: 10–15 mm for optimal light penetration
- **Mixing**: Airlift system with sterile‑filtered air (0.2 µm) at 0.3–0.5 vvm (volume gas per volume medium per minute)

## 3. Growth Medium
- **Basal medium**: Tris‑Acetate‑Phosphate (TAP) for heterotrophic growth; Sueoka’s High‑Salt Medium (HSM) for photoautotrophic growth.
- **Nutrient composition** (for 1 L TAP medium):
  - Tris base: 2.42 g
  - Acetic acid (glacial): 1 mL
  - NH₄Cl: 0.375 g
  - MgSO₄·7H₂O: 0.1 g
  - CaCl₂·2H₂O: 0.05 g
  - K₂HPO₄: 0.108 g
  - KH₂PO₄: 0.056 g
  - Trace elements (Hunter’s mix): 1 mL
- **pH adjustment**: 7.2–7.5 with NaOH/HCl
- **Sterilization**: Autoclave at 121 °C for 20 min (or filter‑sterilize for heat‑labile components).

## 4. Inoculum Preparation
1. Streak algae stock on TAP‑agar plate.
2. Incubate under continuous light (50 µmol photons m⁻² s⁻¹) at 25 °C for 5–7 days.
3. Pick a single colony and inoculate 50 mL of liquid medium in a 250 mL Erlenmeyer flask.
4. Grow under the same light/temperature conditions with orbital shaking (120 rpm) until mid‑log phase (optical density at 750 nm, OD₇₅₀ ≈ 0.8–1.2).
5. Use this culture as inoculum for the photobioreactor at 10 % (v/v) seeding density.

## 5. Cultivation Conditions
- **Temperature**: 25 ± 1 °C (controlled with water jacket or incubator)
- **Light intensity**: 100–150 µmol photons m⁻² s⁻¹ (photosynthetically active radiation, PAR)
- **Light source**: Cool‑white fluorescent tubes or LED panels (peak at 680 nm for PS II)
- **Photoperiod**: Continuous illumination for maximal biomass accumulation.
- **CO₂ supplementation**: 2–5 % (v/v) CO₂ in air, delivered through the airlift system.
- **Dissolved oxygen (DO)**: Maintain < 80 % saturation to avoid photoinhibition.

## 6. Monitoring Growth
- **Daily measurements**:
  - OD₇₅₀ (spectrophotometer)
  - Cell count (hemocytometer or automated counter)
  - Dry weight (filter 10 mL culture through pre‑weighed 0.45 µm membrane, dry at 80 °C for 24 h)
  - pH (adjust with sterile NaOH/HCl if needed)
  - Nutrient depletion (NO₃⁻, PO₄³⁻) using test kits or HPLC.

## 7. Harvesting
- When OD₇₅₀ reaches 2.5–3.0 (late‑log phase), stop aeration and illumination.
- Concentrate cells by centrifugation (5000 × g, 10 min, 4 °C) or tangential‑flow filtration.
- Wash cells once with sulfur‑deprived medium (TAP‑S) if proceeding directly to hydrogen production.
- Pellet can be stored at 4 °C for up to 24 h before hydrogen induction.

## 8. Troubleshooting
- **Slow growth**: Check light intensity, temperature, and contamination.
- **Foaming**: Reduce aeration rate or add anti‑foam agent (silicone‑based, sterile).
- **Precipitation**: Ensure proper mixing and avoid phosphate‑calcium precipitates by adding phosphate after cooling.
- **Contamination**: Use strict sterile technique; if contaminated, discard culture and sterilize the bioreactor.
